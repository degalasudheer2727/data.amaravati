Trust, Security & Governance

Right data, right authority.

Every dataset is graded across four tiers, from Open to Confidential. Your authority — not your team — decides what you can reach, the controls applied, the consent required and the audit trail kept.

Data Exchange Hub · governed by CRDA

Government data, exchanged under governance.

The hard part of open data isn't publishing — it's the governed exchange of sensitive data between agencies. One place for every government entity to request and provide data, with CRDA as the governing authority: classifying data, approving sensitive and confidential exchanges, holding the register of agreements and auditing every transaction.

The governed workflow

From request to provision.

Six governed steps, with CRDA presiding over classification, approval and audit.

Participating entities

A network of providers & consumers.

Departments, utilities and statutory bodies join the hub as data providers, consumers or both — with CRDA presiding over the network.

The exchange register

Live inter-agency agreements.

Every government-to-government exchange, its confidentiality class and where it sits in CRDA governance — open, under review, on agreement, or declined.

Stakeholder performance · weekly KPIs

Every entity, scored on its KPIs.

Each participating entity is measured weekly against its data-exchange KPIs — on-time publishing, exchange-SLA fulfilment, feed freshness, response time and governance compliance — and given an overall score and grade. KPI performance is restricted: sign in as an entity stakeholder to see your own entity's weekly scorecard.
