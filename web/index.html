The data layer · 13 connectors

Every domain of the city, as a live connector.

Each connector publishes governed, refreshable feeds with a defined schema, owner and cadence — the API surface of the digital twin, from land-use zoning to the Krishna riverfront.

The governed data catalogue

What each entity can actually request.

A pre-loaded sample of the governed datasets published across the city's connectors. Every dataset carries a format, an update cadence, an owning department and a confidentiality class that decides who may request it. Pick a class or a domain to filter.

Data classification & clearance

Right data, right authority.

Not everything is open — and that's the point. Every dataset is graded across four tiers, from Open to Confidential. Your authority, not your team, decides what you can reach, the controls applied, the consent required, and the audit trail kept.

Data Exchange Hub · governed by CRDA

Government data, exchanged under governance.

The hard part of open data isn't publishing — it's the governed exchange of sensitive data between agencies. data.amaravati gives every government entity one place to request and provide data, with CRDA as the governing authority: classifying data, approving sensitive and confidential exchanges, holding the register of agreements and auditing every transaction. Modelled on mature city data-exchange platforms.

Participating entities

A network of providers & consumers.

Departments, utilities and statutory bodies join the hub as data providers, consumers or both — with CRDA presiding over the network.

Concept prototype — not an official product. data.amaravati is an independent concept inspired by the public Amaravati master plan and the spirit of CRDA's data-governance vision. It is not a Government of Andhra Pradesh or APCRDA product and is not endorsed by either.
